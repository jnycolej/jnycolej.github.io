Introduction

In the world of casino card games, blackjack holds a special place due to its simplicity and strategic elements. Blackjack Rules:

  • Overview: Get acquainted with the objective of the game, which is to have a hand total closer to 21 than the dealer's hand without exceeding 21.
  • Card Values: Understand the values assigned to different cards, including numbered cards, face cards (King, Queen, Jack), and the versatile Ace.
  • Gameplay: Learn about placing bets, receiving initial cards, and the various player actions such as hitting, standing, doubling down, and splitting (not implemented in the showcased code).
  • Dealer's Turn: Discover the house rules that dictate the dealer's actions, including hitting on hand totals of 16 or less and standing on hand totals of 17 or more.
  • Winning and Losing: Gain insights into different scenarios for determining wins, losses, and ties, including busts, natural blackjack, and comparing hand totals.

Implementation of a Blackjack Game:

Code Overview

When coding for this project I went with a simplistic CSS/Bootstrap design because I wanted to focus on the actual gameplay. The code for this project is mainly written in JavaScript with HTML being used for the main page and in event handlers to relay crucial game infomation. When implementing the buttons I relied on innerHTML in order for the page to respond to clicks that the user commits during the gameplay instead of using a separate view.js file.

All of the gameplay code is located in round.js. This file is initiated whenever the user loads or reloads the website. Once initiated the program uses Fetch API to retrieve a shuffled deck of cards from the Deck of Cards API (https://deckofcardsapi.com) and deal to the dealer and the player. When programming the game initially I had both cards showing for the dealer and the player. However, this wasnt ideal as traditionally the dealer's second card is hidden until the player stands. In order to simulate this gameplay I dealt the dealer one card while dealing the player two cards. This allowed the game to resemble the traditional gameplay seen in real life.

Explain the integration of a card API (Deck of Cards API) to fetch and display the card images in the game.

Game Flow

  1. The initial game loads immediately upon the window loading
  2. The dealer is dealt one card and the player is dealt two
  3. The player has to decide whether to hit or not
    • If the player hits another card is drawn from the deck and their hand total is increased.
      • If the player gets 21 then they win
      • If the player goes over 21 then the lose (or bust)
    • If the player stands then the dealer begins to draw cards. The dealer draws until they reach 17 or 21.
      • If the dealer is at 17 they stay
      • If the dealer is at 21 they win
      • If the dealer is under 17 they must draw until they are over 17
  4. After the dealer recieves all their cards and they havent bust or hit 21 then the players hand and the dealers hands are compared. Whichever one has the most value wins.
  5. If the player wishes to play again they click the new game button
